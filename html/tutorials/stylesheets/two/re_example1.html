 Recursion by Example: Introduction
Chad Mazzola

If you know anything about WildeWare, you know that we love recursion. Sure, using a loop is probably much easier and probably much more memory efficient, but recursion is just plain more fun. And isn't that the point?

Besides being cool, recursion is also very difficult. Just about any program that uses recursion requires that you give a few extra minutes of study before you can fully understand it. More complex examples of recursion may require significant study before you can even start to grasp them. But the key is to expose yourself to lots of examples and try to "think" recursively, which can be quite difficult since our natural thinking process is usually more geared to the iterative structure used in loops.

To get your mind in the mood for recursion we will examine several interesting examples of recursive functions, written in C++, that should provide good examples of the sort of problems recursion is useful in solving.

Let's dive into the first example…FIBONACCI NUMBERS!
