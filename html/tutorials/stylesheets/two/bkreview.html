WildeWare Book Review

I happen to think the mark of a true programming fanatic is the amount of saliva that is produced while they browse the aisles at Barnes & Noble. Looking at all those shiny books with their fresh white pages, just waiting to be picked up and obsessed over, it's one of life's few pure pleasures. But nothing's worse than shelling out $40 for a book that looks great, and then a week later realizing that it hardly does what it promised.

So what we want to do is help fellow programmers know what books to avoid, what books are a must buy, and what books are just a run of the mill overview of a topic. If you have any opinions (good, bad or indifferent) on a programming book then take some time to fill out the form below. Your help could spare the next guy the injustice of forking over money for a brand new paper weight.
