VERTICAL MARQUEE

During your romps through the internet you’ve probably seen more than a few text scrollers made in Java. Personally, I think they stink (Uh...except for that one that Teto made of course). Why should I sit there waiting for an applet to load, and then have the whole page respond slowly, just so I can have “magical” scrolling text.

If you have the incurable desire to put a scroller on your page, here is a much better option. By setting the direction parameter on the dreaded <marquee> tag to UP rather than left or right, you suddenly have a vertical scroller. Not only do you not have to wait for an applet to load, but you can have ANY content at all(such as images or links) in this scroller instead of being limited to just text. If you consider yourself an avant-garde designer (or an idiot) you could even nest multiple <marquee> tags inside of each other. You can also gain additional control over your scroller by stopping and starting the scroller. For instance, any time the user puts their cursor in the marquee you could stop it, and when they move the cursor out, start it again.

There are a ton of ways to modify the marquee tag, but here is a fairly basic example.

<marquee behavior="scroll" direction="up" height="250" scrolldelay="185" width="30%" bgcolor="silver" onMouseOver="stop()"onMouseOut="start()">

</marquee>
