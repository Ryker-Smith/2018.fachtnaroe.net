Port Status LED is OFF:

The Port Status LED may be off because there is no equipment connected to the port or there is a problem with the connected equipment.

The problem could be caused by any of the following:

  1. The connected PC is not powered up.
  1. The connected laptop is either not powered up or in sleep mode (conserving energy).
  1. The cable between the connected equipment and the OfficeConnect equipment is either:
    1. Disconnected — check that the ends are securely connected to the PC and hub.
    2. Broken — replace the cable with a cable you know to be working.
    3. Wrong type — a common mistake is to use telephone cables.
  1. If the PC is connected to the highest numbered port on the OfficeConnect device, the Uplink/Normal (MDI/MDI-X) switch may be in the incorrect position. Toggle Uplink/Normal (MDI/MDI-X) switch to see if the Port Status LED comes on.

  2. If you have just installed the PC, the NIC or PCMCIA card may not be correctly seated in the slot. Re-install the NIC or PCMCIA card.

  3. If you have just installed the NIC or PCMCIA card, the driver may be incorrectly installed or there may be a driver conflict. Remove and re-install the driver.

  4. The NIC or PCMCIA card may be faulty. If there is a fault, refer to the NIC or PCMCIA user guide for more information.

  5. A Fast Ethernet port is connected to an Ethernet NIC or PCMCIA card.

  6. A 10/100 NIC or PCMCIA card is not correctly configured.

  7. An Ethernet hub has been connected to a Fast Ethernet hub or a hub that does not automatically sense the speed/duplex of the link.
