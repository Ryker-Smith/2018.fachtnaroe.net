6 Network interface products

6.1 Network Interface Cards - for desktops

3Com's award winning EtherLink 10 ISA, 10 PCI, and 10/100 PCI network interface cards (NICs), as well as the newer OfficeConnect and USB Network Interface, are unbeatable in reliability, compatibility and performance.

NICs are usually differentiated by the type of Ethernet connection they provide, for example Ethernet, Fast Ethernet or Coaxial. The type of connector that they plug into inside the PC can also differentiate them. All PCs have several expansion sockets, the two most common types are ISA and PCI:

  • ISA (Industry Standard Architecture) sockets have been standard since the first PC and are still used in modern PCs because of the huge range of expansion cards designed for this socket. ISA sockets are about 14cm long. 3Com ISA cards support 10Mbps performance only.
  • PCI (Peripheral Component Interconnect) sockets are used in all Pentium desktop PCs. PCI sockets provide a much higher performance than ISA, this is essential to get the most out of modern high performance expansion cards. PCI sockets are about 9cm long. 3Com PCI cards support either 10Mbps or 10/100Mbps networking.

For information on Wireless NICs see Wireless products.
