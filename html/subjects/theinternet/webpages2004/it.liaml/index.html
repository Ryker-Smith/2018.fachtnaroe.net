Where did the internet come from?

The internet timeline begins in 1962, before the word internet was even invented. The Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) of the U.S. Department of Defence lays the groundwork for ARPANET. The ARPANET is basically the forerunner of today´s Internet. (ARPA) awarded to BBN the contract to build a network of four Interface Message Processors located at different sites. Information could be passed on from one computer to another by using the ARPANET system.The network expanded to twenty three sites by April 1972 and thus gave rise to today´s booming market of Internet technologies. However, it was Ray Tomlinson, a computer engineer who was working for the company that invented ARPANET, who made great strides in advancing the Internet. Ray Tomlinson sent the first message from one computer to another across a network in 1971, the computers were literally side by side. Tomlinson, by combining the SNDMSG program, which allowed users of the same computer to leave messages for one another, and the CYPNET program that would allow users to send files to remote users linked to the ARPANET, had created the technology to leave messages for users in remote locations on the ARPANET network. BBN proved that packetizing data was not only but that it could be applied to high spead networks transmitting messages across varied routes to dispersed destinations - the concept underlying today´s Internet.In 1990 the ARPANET was retired. Due to the explosion of Internet use the U.S. Gouvernment had to transfer management to independent organizations in 1995.


Where did the World Wide Web come from?

In 1980, Tim Berners-Lee, wanted to easily access his saved phone numbers and documents he wrote, they were both saved in different databases. He created a hypertext computer program that allowed him to make the connections. Later, he developed a program that could link databases around the world. He named it the “World Wide Web”. When he distributed his software in 1991 other computer whizzes immediately developed the idea.


Who makes the intenet work?

Rather than saying who makes the internet work we should concentrate on what makes the internet work. The unique thing about the Internet is that it allows many different computers to connect and talk to each other. This is possible because of a set of standards, known as that govern the transmission of data over the network: (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol).
