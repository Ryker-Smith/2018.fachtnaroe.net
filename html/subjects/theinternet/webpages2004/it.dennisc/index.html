Where Did The Internet Come From?


    DEFINITION

The Internet is a network of computer networks and individual computers all linked together by coaxial cable, telephone lines or satellite links. This network allows people to access information and communicate with other computer users globally.


    THE INTERNET


The Internet was developed in 1970 by ARPANET- Advanced Research Projects Agency of the U.S. Department of Defence. It was designed to allow the scientific community to communicate with one another. When it was created it was reportedly designed to withstand a nuclear attack that would normally have cut off other forms of communication such as telephones. In fact it was self-healing, that is to say that if one part was shut down the routers rerouted traffic around the shutdown and effectively fixed itself. Because of the success of ARPANET every university in the country wanted to sign up. This success meant that ARPANET became difficult to manage, particularly with the large and growing number of university sites on it.


It was broken into two parts:

MILNET, which had the military sites.

ARPANET, newer and smaller, which had the non-military sites.


The computer and networks comprising the Internet all speak the same language, TCP/IP-Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol. People using the Internet however speak many different languages, though English is considered the official language of the Internet. Although other languages are being used more often on the Internet, English still predominates. It is expected that within the decade that English will no longer dominate the Internet. Many of the search engines now have an automatic translating utility that allows users to translate text on the Web from one language to another.


    WORLD WIDE WEB


The World Wide Web-or WWW as it is more commonly referred to- is a section of the Internet where information is linked to other related information, allowing the user to jump from Web Site to Web Site or page to page. The Web is the most popular area of the Internet which accounts for more than 90% of all Internet usage. It is also the fastest growing segment of the Net. From only 130 websites in 1993 , there are now over 1 billion websites.


    IN CONCLUSION


The Internet and the World Wide Web have revolutionised computer and communications around the world like nothing before and can be considered one of the greatest inventions of our time. These worldwide media allow interaction with people without regard for geographic location and they are a great exchange and source of data, information and software. The formation and growth of the Internet has changed the way we do business, communicate, entertain, retrieve information and even educate ourselves to such an extent that it is hard to imagine life without it.
