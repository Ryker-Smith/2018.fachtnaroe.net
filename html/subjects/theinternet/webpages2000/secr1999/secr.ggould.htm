A HISTORY OF THE INTERNET Gemma Gould

In 1973, the US Defence Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) began a research program to investigate techniques and technologies for interlinking networks of various kinds. Their aim was to develop communication protocols which allow networked computers to communicate across multiple linked networks. This was called the Interneting project and the system of networks which emerged from the research program was known as the ''Internet''. The system of protocols which developed over the course of this research program became known as the TCP/IP protocol suite, after the two initial protocols developed: Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and Internet Protocol (IP).

 

In 1986, the US National Science Foundation (NSF) began the development of the NSFNET which, today, provides a major backbone communication service for the Internet.
