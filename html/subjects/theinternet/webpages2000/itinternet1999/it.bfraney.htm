History and Structure of the Internet

1957

In the year 1957 the USSR launched Sputnik which was a Satellite. In response to the launch the USA forms the advanced projects agency in the department of defense to build a lead in the science and technology which was only applicable to military.

1962

In 1962 RAND Paul Baran was hired by the U.S. Air Force to study how would be able to maintain control over missiles and bombers that they have launched. He came up with a packet switched network. Packet switching was the breaking down of data into databases that are labeled to indicate the origin and destination and forwarded to different computers.

USERS: 0

In 1969 a network was constructed linking four nodes.

In 1972 Ray Tomlinson created the first e-mail program.

1973

In 1973 development on a protocol TCP/IP, it was developed by Vinton Cerf from and Bob Kahn.

This allowed computers to interconnect and communicate with each other.

1976

In 1976 Dr. Robert M. Metcalfe develops Ethernet, which allowed cable to move data extremely fast.

SATNET, Atlantic packet Satellite network. This linked the U.S.A. and Europe. The Satellites were owned by International telecommunications Satellite Organization.

In 1981 National Science Foundation created backbone called CSNET 56 Kps network. Vinton Cerf proposed a plan for an inter-network connection between CSNET and ARPANET.

MILNET was to be used by the military and ARPANET was to support advance research component.

STRUCTURE

INTERNET ENGINEERING TASK FORCE

The IETF is a network of designers, operators, venders and researchers concerned with the Internet architecture. It is accessible to any person.

The technical work is done in groups.

The groups are grouped into areas and managed by area directors. The IAB adjudicates appeals when someone complains.

The IANA is the central coordinator for the assignment of parameter values. The IANA is chartered by the ISOC to act as the clearinghouse and coordinate the use of Internet protocols.

INTERNET SOCIETAL TASK FORCE

ISTF is for the opportunity to make the Internet positively for people who may be looking for work and for countries whose economy is on the rise.

The purpose of ISTF is to describe that the Internet is for every one. In ISTF as a member there would be discussions online.

Topics: Accessibility, Education, Economics, Regulations and Taxation.
