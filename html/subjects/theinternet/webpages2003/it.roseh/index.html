Where did the Internet come from?

The Internet is a network of computers linked together worldwide.

The Internet started in America in the sixties. The United States government were worried that the Russians were going to attack. They were worried if they attacked they would destroy the computer data. In those days computers were big and they filled a whole room. The Internet used to be called ARPANET this stands for Advanced Research Projects Administration, which is now DARPA the added D is for Defence. The US department of defence set up ARPANET in the late 60’s. ARPANET started out small with 3 computer in California one of which was in Utah. ARPANET soon became widely successful because of this success the every university wanted to sign up. The ARPANET became difficult to manage so it was broken into two parts.

  1. Milnet which had military sites
  2. The new smaller ARPANET, which had the non-military sites.

IP = Internet protocol this enables traffic to be routed from one network to another. This also enables people to send electronic mail back and forth to each other. The United States government’s goal was to secure communications between supercomputer labs. They used it to research military missions however in the late 1980’s the Internet was taken over by the National Science Foundation. Their goal was to complete connection between supercomputer sites. Today the Internet is being used everywhere in offices, companies, homes, banks, libraries and schools and general information. The Internet has grown a lot sense the 1960’s. Back in the 1960’s not much people knew bout it but nearly everyone nowadays know what it is and how to use it.
