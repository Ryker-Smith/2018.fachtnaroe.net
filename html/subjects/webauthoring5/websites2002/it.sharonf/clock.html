The Clock

The clock on St. Anne's church was one of the first public clocks in Cork city. Councillor Delay proposed the idea for the clock at a meeting of Cork Corporation in 1843. He was supported by some of the medical profession as they argued that many poor people were in danger of poisionong themselves by not knowing the times when prescribed medication should be taken. Many working class people were unable to tell the time as most of them did not own watches or clocks.
In May 1843 it was agreed that £250 be provided for the design and constructionof the clock. James Mangan, assisted by his son, was chosen to design the clock. Daniel Thresher built the clock at a cost of £18!In 1847, the clock was installed in the church.

Some interesting facts...

  • The dials are 15 feet, 8 inches in diameter.
  • The four sets of hands weigh 5 cwt.
  • The frame of the clock is 14 feet, 6 inches long, 4 feet wide and 5 feet high and weigh two tons and a half!
  • The striking hammer weighs 100lbs
  • The chime hammers,four in total, weigh 26lbs each
  • The pendulum is 14 feet long, the ball weighing 3cwt.

    The Four Faced Liar

    The clock was known to the Corkonians as the "Four Faced Liar" on account of the time being different on each face duringthe hour. The reason for this was that the faces are made of wood and glided, some of the wood is thicker than others ans so some hands stick when they reach these thick numbers, but on the hour they all come together.
