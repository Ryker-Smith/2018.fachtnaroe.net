11th hole
(Championship 449yd Par 4)

One of the great par 4s in Irish golf, the tee is situated right on the cliff edge with the Atlantic crashing
against the rocks far below. The tee shot is intimidating: a slice winds up in the sea and a hook in the dunes
and thick rough. But a good drive sets up a magnificent challenge for the second, almost certainly with a
wood. The dunes continue down both sides of the fairway so thread your shot between them to reach the green
- one of the few at Ballybunion that draws the ball in. There's no need for bunkers at this hole - it's tough
enough without them.
