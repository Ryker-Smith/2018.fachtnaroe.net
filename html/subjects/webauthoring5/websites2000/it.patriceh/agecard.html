Minister John O'Donoghue signs Age Card Regulations

Major initiative underway to combat underage drinking

The Minister for Justice, Equality and Law Reform, Mr. John O'Donoghue, T.D., today announced that he has signed the Intoxicating Liquor Act, 1988 (Age Card) Regulations, 1999. The Regulations will come into operation in 3 months time.

The Regulations provide for the first time a voluntary, national age card scheme which will greatly strengthen the existing measures aimed at curbing the sale of alcohol to persons under 18 years of age. When the Regulations come into operation any person who has attained 18 years of age will be entitled to apply for an age card at his or her local Garda Station. The application will be accompanied by a birth certificate, at least one other document which confirms the identity of the applicant, two photographs and the fee, which will be £5. If everything is in order the Gardaí will authenticate the identity and age of the applicant and forward what is necessary to the Garda Community Relations Section where the card will be made.

The Minister said that the card would only contain the information necessary for it to confirm the holder's age; that is a photograph of the holder of the card along with his or her name and date of birth. The Minister added that any security measures necessary to prevent forging, tampering with or altering, would also be a feature of the card.

The Minister went on to say that he was satisfied that the introduction of a national age card would make a significant contribution to the continuing fight against underage drinking. He was confident that publicans, off-licence holders and others who sold alcohol to the public would fully play their part in ensuring the success of the scheme. In future there could be no excuse for selling alcohol to those who were underage. This would be recognised in the intoxicating liquor legislation which he intends to publish later this year; the defence available to a licence holder to prove that he or she had reasonable grounds for believing that a person was over 18 years of age will be abolished.

Finally, the Minister paid tribute to the Garda Síochána, vintners and the many other people who had organised, funded and operated voluntary identity card schemes around the country for the past number of years. While they operated with varying degrees of success, they lacked the level of consistency and use of the most modern technology that will be a feature of the national scheme.

20 January, 1999.
