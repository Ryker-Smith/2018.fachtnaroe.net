What causes cancer?




In cancer, cells go wrong as a result of faults in their genes, which means that there are mistakes in the cell's instructions.

Genetic faults can either be inherited from our parents, or more often they are the result of damage by cancer causing agents such as tobacco, viruses, sunlight or dietary factors that we encounter from day to day.

It takes more than one fault to turn a cell into a cancer cell, but as we go through life we tend to accumulate these genetic mistakes.

Cancer-causing agents that damage genes are known as carcinogens.
