Breast Cancer





Risk Factors:

There are multiple possible risk factors and amongst these are:

- Advancing age
- First degree relative of a pre-menopausal woman who developed breast cancer has an increased risk to developing the           disease
- Late childbearing on first pregnancy, i.e. over 30 years
- Early menarche - late menopause
- Obesity
- Alcohol
 

Protective Factors:

- First pregnancy at an early age - before 25 years - is highly protective
- High fibre diet
- High intake of fruit and vegetables
 

Signs and Symptoms:
 

  • Breast lump
  • Change in size/shape of breast
  • Dimpling or puckering of the breast
  • Bleeding discharge from the nipple
  • Change in appearance of nipple, i.e. becoming pulled inwards or inverted


Investigations & Diagnosis:

Biopsy:  Removal of breast tissue for examination
Mammography:  Soft tissue x-ray of breasts
Ultra Sound: Ultrasonic examination to determine if a lump is solid or fluid filled
Aspiration:  Removal of fluid from lump in the breast may result in disappearance of the lump

Tests for Spread:
        Bone and liver ultrasound
        Chest x-ray
        Blood test - CA 153

Treatment:

Lumpectomy:  Removal of lump
Segmentectomy:  Removal of tissue surrounding lump
Quadrantectomy: Removal of wide section of tissue surrounding lump
 

The above procedures may be followed by:

  • Radiotherapy:
  • Chemotherapy:  Administration of anti-cancer drugs.
  • Palliative:  Symptom control.
  • Mastectomy:  Removal of the breast.  Lymph glands are either explored or removed at surgery.
  • Hormone: Course of Tamoxifen is usually recommended for a period of two to five years in post menopausal women.


Prognosis: (Outlook)
Is good if diagnosed early.
 
 

Facts & Figures

One in 13 Irish women will develop breast cancer in their lifetime.
One in 11 women over the age of 50 years will develop breast cancer.

In 1996 there were over 1,600 cases of breast cancer of which 1,582 were female breast cancer cases, and 19 were male.  This was an increase on the figures from the year before *.

Breast cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths in women in Ireland.  It accounts for nearly 15% of female deaths from cancer.  Among the European countries, Ireland ranks second in breast cancer deaths.

* Source:    National Cancer Registry
"Cancer in Ireland 1996, Incidence  and Mortality"
