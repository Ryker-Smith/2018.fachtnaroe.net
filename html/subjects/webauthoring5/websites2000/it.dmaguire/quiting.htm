Quitting

     10 tips to prepare you to quit
1. Be committed, make sure that you are ready to work hard to quit.

2.  Discuss the different nicotine replacement therapy options and how to deal with the urges to smoke again.

3.  Set a quit date.

4. On the day you quit smoking, throw away all your cigarettes, matches, lighters, and ashtrays. Visit the dentist and get your teeth cleaned or whitened so that all the tobacco stains are gone. Notice how good your teeth look and persuade yourself to keep them that way.

5. Estimate how much you spent in a week on cigarettes then buy something more productive with the money.

6. For the first few days after you quit, spend as much time away from places that contain smoke.

7. Try to avoid alcohol and other drinks that you associate with smoking.

8. If you miss having something in your mouth, try chewing gum or hard sweets.

9. Change your habits to make smoking hard, it is very difficult to smoke while you are swimming, jogging, at the gym, or any other sport.
 

10. Finally, never allow yourself to think that one cigarette won't hurt.
