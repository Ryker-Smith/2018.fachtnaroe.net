What is in a cigarette.
Nobody knows for sure exactly what ingredients are mixed in with the tobacco. The reason for this is because cigarettes aren’t regulated like food or drink. We only have to trust the tobacco companies word. The same people who under oath swore that nicotine wasn’t addictive. In recent tests carried out in the US by the Federal Trades Commission over 4000 chemicals were found in a cigarette and the smoke from the burning cigarette. Here are just a few of those chemicals found:
 
Carbon Monoxide
This is the odourless and colourless gas that comes out of the exhaust pipe of your car or from a leaking radiator. In large doses it is deadly and in small doses it causes shortness of breath and increased blood pressure.

Tar
Tar is a dark substance that carries the nicotine to the lungs. It also carries a lot of other harmful chemicals, which were found during the FTC testing.

Benzene and Radon
These chemicals have been proven to cause cancer. Health officials have advised against having these chemicals in or around the household so I should think inhaling them regularly couldn’t be good for you.

Nicotine
 Nicotine is the drug responsible for making cigarettes so addictive. Studies have shown nicotine to be as addictive as heroin and cocaine. It only takes nicotine 7 seconds to reach the brain. It triggers various responses in your body; your heartbeat and breathing rate go up and blood vessels constrict slowing down the circulation.
