Effects of Smoking.

Here are just a few of the harmful effects caused by smoking cigarettes:

Lung Diseases
Smoking is the cause of about 80 – 90% of deaths resulting from lung disorders and 85% of all lung cancer deaths. Once damage is done to the lungs it is irreversible. The person could give up smoking to prevent their condition from getting worse but it wont make them better.

A right lung with a shadow.

Heart Disease
Cigarette smoking accounts for 30% of all heart disease deaths. The carbon monoxide in cigarettes increases the amount of cholesterol clogging the arteries. Cigarette smoke also causes the walls of the arteries to stiffen increasing the chances of the arteries rupturing. The nicotine in cigarettes can raise your blood pressure, heart rate, and the oxygen demand for muscles, especially the heart (which is a muscle). Smokers’ blood is more prone to clotting than a non-smoker.
 
 

Cancer
Smoking takes a lot of the blame for cancer of the lips, tongue, salivary glands, mouth, larynx, esophagus, and the middle and lower pharynx. It can also be linked directly to the development of stomach and bladder cancer. Though not yet proven, doctors believe smoking is associated with leukemia.

Hormonal Problems
Smoking effects both male and female reproduction systems. It slows down the mobility of sperm in the male system and brings on premature menopause for a smoking woman by an average of 5 years earlier than a non-smoker.

Passive Smoking
A lot of people die every year as result of passive smoking. All the risks associated with smokers apply to non-smokers who either live with a smoker or who are regularly around a smoker. This is because the smoke coming out at the end of a burning cigarette has a lot more, smaller particles, than the smoke inhaled directly by the smoker. These smaller particles go deeper into the lungs and cause a lot more damage. Infants and young children have tender tissues and under no circumstances be around a burning cigarette. It could lead to them developing asthma, bronchitis and even cancer.

Other Effects

- Nicotine has a harmful effect on the stomach making the digestion of food difficult.

-The effect of some medicines is effected in some smokers.

-A woman is 17 times more likely to become hoarse than a non-smoking woman is.

   -Excess facial hair is 7 times more frequent in women who smoke compared to  women who don't smoke.

-Smoking accelerates the ageing process.

-Smoking is believed to be a part of the cause of snoring and sleep apnea (stop breathing while asleep)
