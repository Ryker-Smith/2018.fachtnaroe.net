Who Owns The Internet?


The Internet is a public collaboration. No one person, organization, or group of organizations owns it. As it grew from a small network of four computers used in research for the United States defense establishment into a public system comprised of hundreds of commercial telecommunication networks of all sizes, thousands of institutions, hundreds of thousands of businesses, and millions of individual users.

Since the Internet started, it has opened a wide amount of information available to us at the touch of a button. The main question that everyone asks is “Who owns the Internet”? Although the Internet is available to anyone who has access to it, someone must own the copyright for it.


Originally the Internet was the brainchild of the Department of Defence to communicate with one another during the war. Then researchers began to use it to share research data and information in the Universities in America. As far as i know, nobody has ever claimed copyright for it. Even though it is now in the process of being locked over and being used as global communication.


The most obvious candidates for ownership in the future are some of the major software companies led by the most obvious and most pervasive, Microsoft, which provides 75 percent of the operating systems controlling PC computers. Microsoft virtually holds the keys to the kingdom. Apple Computer, whose software many devoted users consider far more versatile and useful, seems to be losing market share, although Apple claims about 21 percent of those having access to the Internet. There are, of course, other candidates: Sun Microsystems with its Java aplets and a strong software and hardware position in the server market; or Netscape with its Navigator that now captures 84 percent of the browser market.

I suppose when people think of the Internet they think of Microsoft or Bill Gates because they are the main people involved in the Internet. Not many people know that is was used in the war.

You could say in a way that the original founders. The USA government, are the owners of the Internet in a way. They provided the know how and everyone else took it from there and expanded it as they saw fit.

It seems that everyone in their own way owns a piece of the Internet. People copyright their own websites and web pages.

In turn we will never really know who created the Internet. It was used during the war so the inventors probably wanted to keep a low profile. Its mainly associated with the USA so maybe we should be thanking them in a way for the invention. It has clearly helped mankind in a range of things From communication to business. It also helped create jobs and improved peoples knowledge of the world. Various Internet sites are still unable to determine who is the true developer of the net.
