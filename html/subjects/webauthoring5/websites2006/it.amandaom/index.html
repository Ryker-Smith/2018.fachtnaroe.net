Who Owns the Internet:


Nobody owns the internet well actually everybody owns a piece of the internet. The internet back bone is owned by the National Science Foundation, it is currently run under contract by Merit, a joint venture between IBM and MCI. There registration services are provided by AT&T under contract with the NSF. The least lines that hook the networks together are owned by the regional Bell operating companies and the long distance carriers such as MCI, AT&T, Sprint and WilTEL. So the there is not one owner of the internet we all own it.

The world wide web came from this man pictured above Tim Berners-Lee who invented the world wide web in 1989, the first system was in 1990, he was working at CERN (the European Organization for Nuclear Research). He went on to invent the world wide web-related things such as the HTML and HTTP these are used to transmit web pages between web servers and web browsers so this is how he invented the world wide web. He also wrote the first web client (browser-editor). Tim work in a computer company before going to CERN, he worked in Image Computers Systems, Ferndown, Dorset, England and he was a principal engineer with Plessey Telecommunications, in Poole, England. He is a graduate of Oxford University, he holds 3Com Founders chair at the laboratory for computers science and he directs the World Wide Web Consortium its an open forum of companies and organizations with a goal to lead the web to its full potential.
