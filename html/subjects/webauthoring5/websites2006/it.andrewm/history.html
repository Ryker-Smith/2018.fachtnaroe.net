Where did the Internet come from?


The Internet started in 1969 when the cold war was in operation, the American government created APRANET which was a cold war project when to make a communications network that would not be destroyed by an nuclear attack.


In 1968 apra awarded the APRANET contract to BBN


In1969 the physical network was constructed linking the first 4 nodes of the APRANET between University of California at Los Angeles, SRI (in Stranford at Santa Barbra, and the University of Utha.


In 1972 the first e-mail was created.


In 1974 the first use of the term Internet was used by Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn.


In 1976 Dr. Robert M. Metcalif develops Ethernet which allows the transfer of data at extremely fast rates.


In 1983 the Internet Activities Board (IAB) was created. Also this year the University Of Wisconsin invented the Domain Name System (DNS) this made it easier for people to access other servers.


In 1984 the APRANET was divided into two Networks (MILNET & ARPANET).

MILNET for military use.

ARPANET for advanced research.

Who owns the Internet?


No single person owns the Internet, as the Internet relies on the physical make up that connects the networks to each other. However, there are many corporations, governments, companies and schools that own small pieces of that make up. There are organisations that see to standardise what happens on the Internet to our benifit.
