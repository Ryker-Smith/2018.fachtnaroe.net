Where did the Internet come from?



The Internet started in America in the 60s for military purposes, the Americans were frightened of attack from the Russians, there were very few computers at this time, they were so big they would fill whole rooms, one well planned attack could easily destroy the whole US military system. They decided to build a computer system consisting of several small computers all connected together so that if one machine was destoyed the other ones would be ok. It was initially called ARPANET. Eventually more and more computers were connecting to this network and it began to grow, networks were also developing in other countries and they eventually joined, the Internet as it became known at this time shot up. In 1969 there was only 4 computers connected to the Internet and to date there are 9.5 million!


Although the US were responsible for the setting up of the Internet, they do not actually own the Internet, no one person or organisation actually owns it, in fact the only logical solution is that Everybody owns their own piece. There are however organisations that do oversee what happens on the Internet and assign ip addresses and domain names, such as the National Science Foundation, Icann and Internic. The Internet became a fast and very effective way of communication, knowledge could be shared in a very short space of time. In 1972 Ray Tomlinson created the email program, this was a massive step in making the Internet what it is today. Emails are a fast way of communicating with anyone anywhere in the world but rules must be followed while using emails, security is a major issue with emails and one must always bare in mind that an email may be read by a number of people as well as the person it was sent to. Other means of communication over the Internet to name two are Msn and Yahoo, these are real time methods of communication which allow you to talk to anyone again any where in the world. Although the Internet allowed people to share information with eachother very quickly, the information was not always correct.



In 1990 Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web at Cern, a meeting place for physicists from all over the world. It was a system of text links and multimedia capabilities that made the Internet accessible to mass audiences. The World Wide Web became available on the Internet in 1991. It was a long awaited database of information, with a click of a mouse pages of information were now available and the user could quickly find the exact information they required. The Internet has been designed so that it can never be broken, because it is a network of networks, the fact of its original design makes it impossible.To break the Internet every computer all over the world would have to be destroyed, if one remained the Internet would be intact. Therefore because it would be virtually impossible to destroy all computers In my opinion the Internet cannot be broken.
