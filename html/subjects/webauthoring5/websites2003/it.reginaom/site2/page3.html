How to Reduce the Risk of Developing Skin Cancer

The most effective sunscreen is clothing, so cover up in the sun. Hats and baseball caps protect your head from the sun. Wear a T-shirt or top in the midday sun. Protect your eyes from the sun by wearing sunglasses Use a sunscreen with a sun protection factor (SPF) 15 or higher with UVA and UVB protection and re-apply every two hours on your face and on all exposed skin. Between 11 a.m. and 3 p.m. is the most dangerous time for sunlight. Take special care to protect children. The Irish Cancer Society recommends that you avoid the use of sunbeds and sunlamps
