VEHICLE GROWTH

The total number of vehicles has increased by more than 50%. The total number of vehicles increased by54% during the 10-year period between 1988 and 1998. Private cars accounted for the majority part of the increase. But heavy goods vehicles as well increased by 58% in the period. Rising vehicles number and increased usage of cars have posed a major environmental challenge.

ENERGY GROWTH

The country’s total primary energy requirement has increased by more than one –third. Energy is recognized generally as a critical sector relating to the environment. Impacts on the environment arise at each operational phase of the energy system: production, transmission, transformation, distribution and consumption. The Green Paper on sustainable energy gives an overview of energy trends between 1980-2010.

TOURISM AND TRADE

There has been substantial expansion in tourism and trade. Though this is a welcome trend it can also have an adverse impact on our environment. Many of the visitors coming to Ireland do so to enjoy the country’s environment. If not managed properly, growing tourist numbers can harm the very environment that attracts them.

It can be seen from the above that Ireland’s economic boom has brought dramatic increase in consumer spending, house building, traffic volume and energy use. There are growing concerns that all this is affecting the environment. Efforts have to be made that Ireland’s growing richness should not affect its environment.
