What happens when you have asthma attack?

 

Asthma is a disease, in which inflammation of the airways, which stops the airflow going into and out of the lugs. When you have an asthma attack your muscles of the bronchial tree becomes tight which causes the lining of the air passages to swell out. Because of the swelling you start to hear a whezzing sound. But most people mightened have a whezzing sound they could suffer from shortness of breathing. Or they could just have the attack without any signs.

A person with asthma their attacks can last minutes or some times days it depends on how bad the person gets an asthma attack. And can become dangerous if the airflow becomes severely restricted. With asthma you can trigger an attack by inhaling allergens such as pet’s dander, dust mites, tobacco smoke, these allergens would only affect sensitive people with asthma.

How to prevent asthma attacks

 

To prevent asthma attacks you could encasing mattresses and pillows, in allergen – impermeable covers, removing carpets from bedrooms and also can be reduced by lowering indoor heat. If the person is allergic to an animal and the animal cannot be removed from the home you should keep the animal outside at all times. Filtering material can be placed over the heating outlets to trap animal dander. And if the person is exposed to cigarette smoke the air pollution industrial dusts, and irritating fumes should also be avoided. Also to help the person with asthma there are medication you can use. Which you can get of your GP.
