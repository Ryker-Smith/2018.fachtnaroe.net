PROFESSIONAL DECORATING
Preparation of Walls and Ceilings when the existing surface is:

Wallpaper
When removing wallpaper you will need a scraper, a 4" paint brush and a bucket of water.  Removing wallpaper can be very easy or can end up being a very hard and time consuming job.  To start off try and remove the top layer of paper.  The best way to do this is to lift the two bottom corners of the paper with a scraper and try to peel away the whole layer of paper in one sheet.  When top layer of paper has been removed starting from the top right hand side of the wall wash down with the brush and water.  Completely soak the paper with water and allow to soak in for a short time.  Using a scraper and starting at the bottom of the wall scrap away as much paper as possible.  Any stubborn areas may need to be soaked more than once.  It is often advised in DIY books and magazines to score the paper but this is wrong..It serves no purpose to score the walls.  Scoring the walls usually ends up with damaging the  plaster  by leaving score marks.  So the best method is a paint brush, a bucket of water and a scraper.

Emulsion paint
When the existing surface is emulsion paint the problem areas to look for are flaking paint or blisters in the paint.  If there are areas like this use a scraper to remove all the loose paint

Gloss or Eggshell paint
As with emulsion check for unsound paint and remove with a scraper.  Wash down with sugar soap to remove grease.

Distemper
Distemper is like a thin layer of chalk which prevents paint adhering to the wall or ceiling.  It can be a nuisance to remove.  The best way is to use a scraper and sandpaper.  Completely remove any loose paint.

Once all the paper or flaking paint has been removed it is then time to rub the walls and ceiling down with sandpaper.  It is very important to rub down as this removes any rough patches and leaves a smooth feel to the walls.  Also when you rub down you will come across any problem areas that you may have missed.  So starting with the ceiling and then working from the tops of the walls down to the tops of the skirting boards rub every thing down including the tops of the skirting boards and edges of door frames and window frames.
