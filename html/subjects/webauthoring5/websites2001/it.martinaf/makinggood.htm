PROFESSIONAL DECORATING
Areas to be made good.

Stains.
You may come across many different types of stains such as smoke, water marks and felt-tip pen.  There are various ways of dealing with stains.  Special Stain Blocks which come in the form of an aerosol can be sprayed over the stained area.  These are very effective over small areas but can work out to be very expensive over a large area.  one of the vest and most inexpensive ways to cover a stain is to paint over it with an oil based paint like Gloss, Undercoat and Eggshell.  One coat of oil based pint is usually enough to blank out the stain completely.

Filling.
Types of filler:
Filler can either be ready mixed or come in powered form which is mixed with water.
Fine Fill which comes as a ready mix is suitable for use on wood work.  It comes in a tub and is a very smooth filler.  Never over fill with ready mix as it is very hard to rub down once it has driedc.
Flexible Filler also known as Caulk comes in a tube witha a nozzle which is used with a special gun.  It is suitable for fillin joints in woodwork such as tops oskirting s and door frames.

There will almost certainly be areas that need filling even on newly plastered walls and ceilings.  Start by filling the ceiling.  When filling large cracks fill the area from the side and then smooth along the length of the crack with the filler knife.  Never over fill it is much better to fill a hole or crack several times.  When the ceiling has been filled it is time to fill the walls.  Start at a top corner of a wall and work around the room in sequence until you end up back at where you started.  Fill all holes,cracks and dents in the surface.

Rubbing Down.
Once all the filling has been finished it is time to rub down the ceiling, walls amd woodwork completely.  Work from the top of the room downwards.  Make sure every thing is rubbed down not just areas that have been filled.

When using any type of scraper never dig into the surface as this will just cause damage such as dents and scratch marks.  The blades should not be blunt and should just scrap over the surface.  When using a scraper or sandpaper on woodwork always work with the grain of the wood.
