PROFESSIONAL DECORATING
Tools, equipment and materials for preparation.
It is important to have the right tools for the right job.  The main tools used in the preparation of a room are scrapers, filling knives, sandpaper, steam strippers, and blow torches.

Scrapers
There are several different type of scrapers that may be used for different stripping jobs.  The flat slightly flexible scrapers such as the Broad-blade and the Narrow-blade are used for scraping flat surfaces such as ceilings, walls and flush doors.  These scrapers come in different sizes such as 4", 3" or 1".  They can be used for removing wallpaper, paint, ceiling tiles as well as raking out cracks. Scrapers for removing paint from mouldings such as door or window frames are called Shave Hooks.  These are either Combination Shave Hook which can be used on rounded mouldings or  Triangular Shave Hook which removes paint from corners of window and door frames.

Skarsten scrapers hold a razor sharp blade and will strip paint back to the bare wood.  They are very good for stripping paint or varnish off hand rails on staircases.

Glass scrapers hold a Stanley blade and are used for removing paint from glass such as window panes or from ceramic tiles.  Always make sure the blade is not blunt as it is very easy to scratch the surface.

Wire brush
A wire brush may also be used for removing loose or flaking paintwork.

Sandpaper
Sandpaper can either come in sheets or on a roll.  Usually the roll of paper is better value for money.  There are different grades such as fine grade, medium grade and coarse grade.  You can also use a sanding block which has different grades of coarsness on each side.  Sanding blocks can also be rinsed out with water and used many times.  The reason for sanding down is to remove any flaking paint, bits of old wallpaper and adhesive, to remove any glaze as well as leaving a smooth finish to the surface.  It also gives the surface a key.  As with using a scraper on wood when sanding down wood always sand with the grain.

Electric Sander
There are a variety of electric sanders including disc sander and drum sander.  Electric sanders are mainly used on wood like floors and doors.  They are not suitable for removing heavy coats of gloss paint as the sand paper clogs up quickly.

Hot Air Gun
Hot air guns are like an extra hot hair dryer.  They are very good for removing varnish.

Blow Torches.
Blow torches soften the paint or varnish.  They use bottles of gas and soften the paint or varnish by melting it from the heat of the flame.  Always be careful when using a blow torch as the hat paint can burn the skin and hot flakes may go into the eyes.  It is always best to wear gloves and safety goggles when using a blow torch.

Steam Strippers.
Steam strippers are used for removing wallpaper and work by softening the adhesive underneath the paper thus making it easy to remove the paper.

Chemical strippers.
Chemical strippers are mainly used on paint or varnish.  They work by dissolving the paint.  They can be very irritating to the skin so care should always be taken when using them as they can burn the skin.

Ladders.
When decorating any kind of room you will almost certainly need to use a ladder.  Ladders can be very dangerous if not used properly.  You should avoid using a chair or table as a substitute for a ladder, these were not designed for standing on and reaching a height.  It is very easy to fall from an insecure ladder or a chair on top of a table.  Always choose the appropriate ladder for the job.  Make sure the ladder is secure on the floor.  Make sure the ladde is high enough for the job.  Never use a ladder if you have to stand on the top rung or if you have to reach further than arms length to the side of the ladder.  It is very easy to lose your balance or to unbalance a ladder which results in falling off.  Never rest tools or equipment on a ladder, if they fall off it can cause serious damage to people below.
