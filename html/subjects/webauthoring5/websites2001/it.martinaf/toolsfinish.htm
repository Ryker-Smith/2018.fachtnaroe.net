PROFESSIONAL DECORATING
Tools, equipment and materials for finishing.

Paintbrushes.
There are a wide variety of paintbrushes on the market and this can make it hard to choose the right brush for the job.  A good brush will always cost what may seem to be expensive but it will be worth it in the long run.  The saying 'you get what you pay for# is very true when it comes to decorating tools.  A good paintbrush should hold the paint well and should be flexible as if  the paint is gliding over the surface.

Emulsion brushes are usually a lot thicker and have longer bristles.  They need to be flexible when loaded with paint.  When cutting in walls and ceilings you have to have a flexible brush so you can get right into any corners and fill with paint.  They come in various sizes, 1/2in, 1in, 2in and 4in.

Oil brushes usually come in 1/2in, 1in,2in and 3in sizes.  The more these brushes are uses the better they perform.  When you have a nes brush it is always better to break it in by using on undercoat or primer before using it on the finish coat.  Soon you will notice the vbrush taking shape and will find it easier to use the more it is used.

All brushes come in natural bristle or synthetic fibre.  Usually natural bristles are best but nowadays some very good synthetic brushes can do the job just as good.

Rollers.
Like brushes, rollers come in many different sizes and textures.  They can cover an area in a fraction of the time it  would take to use a brush.  A roller will also give a nice even finish if applied evenly and the paint is spread out evenly.
