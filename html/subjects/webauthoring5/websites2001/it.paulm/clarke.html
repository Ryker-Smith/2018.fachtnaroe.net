The 1916 Easter Rising

Thomas James Clarke (1857-1916)

was born in the Isle of Wight of Irish parents and brought up in South Africa and Dungannon. He went to America in 1871 and joined Clan na Gael, returning to England on a dynamiting mission in 1883. He was arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment, of which he served fifteen years under servere conditions. He was released in 1898 but unable to find work, returned to the US. His return to Dublin in 1907 was with the deliberate intention of reviving the IRB. A signatory of the Proclamation, he served in the GPO durning Easter Week and was executed with Pearse and MacDonagh on 3 May 1916.
