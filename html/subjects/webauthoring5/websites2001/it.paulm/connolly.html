The 1916 Easter Rising

James Connolly (1868-1916)

was born in Edinburgh of Irish parents. Having been a socialist labour organiser in Ireland and the US, he returned to Ireland in 1910 and was the worker's leader while larkin was imprisoned during the 1913 lock-out. He formed the Citizen Army, convinced that Ireland was ripe for an armed socialist revolt. He was persuaded to join with the IRB and as the leader with some experience was made military commander durning the Rising. His execution on 12 May 1916 marked the end of the judicial killings.
