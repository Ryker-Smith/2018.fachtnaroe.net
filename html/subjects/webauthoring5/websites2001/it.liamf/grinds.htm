How to do the boardslide

 A boardslide is a slide in which the deck is slammed onto the rail/bench/ledge or whatever and

slid along. This is the most common way for skaters to break their decks but it's a great trick

none the less. To do a boardslide approach the rail at a 30 degree angle (or less) and as you

approach the target ollie up. When you feel that your deck is over the object push down with

both feet and slap the board onto the rail. As you would with all other slides, "push" the board

forward with either your heels (frontside) or the ball of your foot (backside). As you near the end of the rail apply a little more weight to your back foot and then move your front foot (and the board) around 90 degrees, drop off and ride away.
