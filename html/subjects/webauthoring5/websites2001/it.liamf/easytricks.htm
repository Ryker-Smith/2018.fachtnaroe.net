Here are all the beginner tricks-these tricks should be attempted only after the ollie has been mastered.

 

How to do the fakie ollie:

To do this you NEED to know how to ollie while moving. The fakie ollie is an ollie in your

regular stance but you need to go backwards (this is a very basic trick). All you need to do is pedal

backwards, get into the ollie position and do and ollie. It only takes about two tries to

accomplish this trick.

How to do a manual:

Put your front foot on the front truck bolts and put your back foot on two of the back bolts.

This stance helps to control your board. Now, you need to have a lot of balance and just ride on

your board and raise the front of your board so you are just balancing and riding on the back

truck.

How to do the nose manual:

This is the same thing as a normal manual but you raise the back truck up so you are riding on

just the front truck.

How to do the ollie manual:

In order to learn the ollie manual, it is painfully obvious that you must learn the ollie, and the

manual first. Damn! An ollie manual is the act of ollieing and landing on your two back wheels and

balancing on them for a distance.

The best way I know to learn this is to pop a tiny ollie, as though you are ollieing over a crack,

and don't level out the board. Land on your back wheels and try to be in the position you are in

when you manual when you land.

One thing I know is that you should not try to learn this in place. Some people learn it moving

fast, some people learn it moving rather slowly, but hardly any can successfully learn it standing

still. You may need to try fast and slow. Experiment with it and have fun. Don't get pissed off if

you don't pull it the first try! Skate Hard.
